About Me

I’ve been working in the Government Technology sector for 12 years. During that time I’ve served in multiple roles, which has allowed me to gain a thorough understanding of both customer, internal team, and business needs. Channeling this knowledge has enabled my success as a Product Manager for the last 10 years. Product Management can be a big umbrella covering many different business needs and I’ve done them all: Product Owner, Scrum Master, Trainer, Sales Engineer, User Researcher, Market Analyst, and even some Software Engineering.

When I’m not at work, you can find me at the ice rink for hockey, outside for a variety of activities like snowboarding, water skiing, camping and hiking, or at an event space for live music and comedy.

Work

The following is a summary of my work history along with some narrative for each role. For more details, please see my LinkedIn page.

OpenGov

Product Manager • August 2019 - March 2020 (Laid off due to Covid-19)

Oversaw the Reporting and Analysis product line. Helped shepherd the NextGen Reporting through general availability. Solicited key customers and stakeholders for requirements and design feedback. Worked closely with the engineering team to deliver on critical features and initiatives.

Granicus

Product Manager • July 2010 - August 2019

Inheriting a suite of existing SaaS products aimed at making the public meeting process for local government clerks and elected officials more transparent and efficient, I oversaw a complete rewrite of the suite including a backend technology upgrade and user interface refresh when I first started in my product management role. No small feat! Today, this product line is worth several million dollars in annual recurring revenue.

After a successful launch and eventual 100% client adoption of the aforementioned product suite, I was tasked with shepherding a brand new product for streamlining the citizen application process for local boards and commissions from a beta phase to full release. Working closely with clients and internal Granicus teams, this included overseeing the development of several new modules as well as many features and fixes. This product is also what sparked my desire to learn code skills. Many of my bug fixes and feature requests are still in the codebase!

Most recently and due to my past product successes, I took over product management duties for a product suite Granicus inherited in an acquisition. Automating the document recording, indexing, and public search process for county clerks and recorders, the product suite accounts for a significant portion of recurring revenue, but has been languishing due to an aging tech stack and outdated user interface. Gathering and analyzing data for total addressable market, competitive landscape, revenue growth projections, profit, and costs, I put together a holistic product view for the CEO and executive team to make an informed decision about how to move forward with the product line.

Deployment Technician • January 2010 - July 2010

Working with customers both onsite and remotely, I deployed new hardware and software installations. Blending technical, communication, project management, and interpersonal skills, I ramped up quickly and was successful from the start due to my previous experience in customer success. Alas, while I enjoyed my responsibilities, I was not in the deployment world for very long.

After demonstrating my aptitude for product management through side projects outside of my main duties in this role as well as my previous one at Granicus, I was promoted to be the first formal product manager at Granicus on the newly formed PM team!

Client Success Manager • August 2008 - January 2010

My first role in my Granicus journey was serving as the primary point of contact for the ever-expanding Pacific region of clients. When customers needed help, they contacted me. Handling non-technical questions with training guidance as well as the occasional frustrated client with gentle guidance, being responsible for the overall success and happiness of our clients was a duty I relished and took pride in doing. As I got a few months further into my tenure, I started tackling my own support and design cases after-hours rather than passing them along to other internal teams. It was during this time that I augmented my communication and training skillset with technical aptitude.

An internal decision was made to re-focus the success team at up-selling existing clients, which was not quite the direction I wanted to go. Recognizing my blend of skills and desire to continue to build my technical knowledge, my manager promoted me to the deployment team.

Marketing Designs

Copy Editor • June 2007 - August 2008

My post-college career path began with a job in copy editing, which requires the utmost attention to detail. I was responsible for catching any and all errors on special marketing materials we produced for real estate agents serving the high end of the Bay Area real estate market (read: multi-million dollar homes). I kept an eye out for spelling and grammatical errors of course, but I also became quite adept at spotting design issues like alignment and missing elements. I achieved a 99.7% accuracy rate on over 2,000 printed projects (calculated based on the number of projects that had to be reprinted due to an error I missed). Pretty darn good, eh?
